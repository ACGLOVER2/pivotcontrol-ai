# Pivot Control Index™ (PCI) — Scoring Framework

The Pivot Control Index™ is PivotControl AI's proprietary scoring system for measuring communication authority in high-stakes enterprise sales conversations.

---

## What the PCI Measures

The PCI does not measure filler words, talk time, or speaking speed. It measures **authority under pressure** — specifically, what happens to a seller's communication structure, framing, and confidence signal when the conversation becomes adversarial, ambiguous, or cognitively demanding.

Enterprise sellers don't lose deals because of bad strategy. They lose momentum during unstructured pivots:

- When an executive interrupts mid-sentence
- When multiple stakeholders press simultaneously
- When a competitor is introduced
- When a VP contradicts something said earlier
- When the conversation goes off-script

The PCI measures how well a seller maintains authority across those moments.

---

## The Five Metrics

### 1. Interruption Recovery (20 points)

**What it measures:** How quickly and cleanly the seller regains structure after being interrupted, corrected, or redirected.

**What good looks like:**
- Returns to the original thread within 1–2 sentences
- Acknowledges the interruption without over-apologizing
- Uses the interruption as a credibility moment rather than a disruption

**What poor looks like:**
- Abandons the original point entirely
- Over-explains or justifies after correction
- Loses pacing and takes more than 3–4 sentences to recover

**Example from beta (Antoine Wade, GDIT):**  
At 9:54, Antoine misquoted the non-production instance count. Rather than moving past it, he said: *"I am a little confused by the 36 non-production instances. Did I not capture that the right way, Steve? That is what happens when I take notes."*  
Score: 17/20. Clean recovery. Turned an error into a collaborative accuracy moment.

---

### 2. Structural Clarity (20 points)

**What it measures:** Whether responses are organized, compressed, and coherent under pressure.

**The pattern it detects:**
- Point → Reason → Example → Bridge (executive structure)
- vs. Context → Background → Detail → More detail → Eventual point (tactical drift)

**What good looks like:**
- Lead with the conclusion, then support it
- No single response runs more than 4 sentences before a pause or check-in
- Transitions signal movement between ideas

**What poor looks like:**
- Over-explanation after a direct question
- Sentence length increases under pressure (a key cognitive overload signal)
- Answers that cover three topics when one was asked

**The 3-sentence rule:** No technical or capability answer should exceed 3 sentences before offering to show or elaborate. Compression signals control.

---

### 3. Strategic Framing (20 points)

**What it measures:** Whether answers stay at the executive/outcome level or drift into tactical, product, or operational detail.

**Executive framing sounds like:**
- "What that means for your team is..."
- "The risk if this isn't addressed is..."
- "That directly affects your cycle time because..."

**Tactical drift sounds like:**
- Explaining how the product works instead of what it does
- Answering the question asked instead of the question behind it
- Moving from business outcome → feature detail under pressure

**The Impact Bridge:** Before adding any technical detail, bridge to business impact first. *"We can integrate with SAP through the API layer — what that means for your inspectors is they stay in SAP doing their actual work instead of switching systems."*

---

### 4. Authority Signal Stability (20 points)

**What it measures:** Whether tone, pacing, and language confidence hold under sustained pressure.

**Signals of authority stability:**
- Pacing stays consistent or slows slightly under pressure
- Voice doesn't rise or accelerate when challenged
- Language stays direct — no hedging, no qualifiers, no defensive phrases

**Authority erosion markers:**
- "I mean..." / "Basically..." / "What I'm trying to say is..."
- "I guess..." / "I'm not sure, but..." / "I think..."
- Answering faster under pressure (the opposite of what authority looks like)
- Defensive tone when a competitor or objection is introduced

**The stability test:** Would you sound the same at minute 5 and minute 55 of a high-pressure executive call? Authority stability means the answer is yes.

---

### 5. Narrative Reclaim (20 points)

**What it measures:** Whether the speaker redirects the conversation when it drifts, or follows the questioner's frame throughout.

**Narrative control looks like:**
- Answering the question, then pivoting back to the core value frame
- Reframing a competitor comparison as a different kind of question
- Using the Reclaim Move: *"That's an important lens — let me tie it back to..."*

**Following the questioner's frame looks like:**
- Spending two minutes defending against a ChatGPT comparison instead of pivoting to deployed proof
- Letting procurement reframe the scope of a deal without anchoring back to the original value
- Answering every question on the questioner's terms without ever redirecting

**The pre-loaded reclaim phrase:** Every seller should have one account-specific reclaim phrase ready before every high-stakes call. It should reference the client's actual situation — not a generic template.

## Score Interpretation

| Range | Level | What It Means |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 80–100 | Executive | Communication authority is consistent and structural across the full call arc. Gaps are narrow and specific. |
| 60–79 | Capable | Strong in known territory. Authority degrades under ambiguity or sustained pressure. Clear development path. |
| 40–59 | Developing | Authority is present in moments but not sustained. Structural patterns need deliberate rebuilding. |
| 0–39 | At Risk | Communication patterns are actively eroding deal confidence and executive perception. Immediate intervention needed. |

---

## What the PCI Is Not

- It is not a measure of product knowledge
- It is not a measure of likeability or rapport
- It is not a filler word counter
- It is not scored against a generic communication rubric

The PCI measures **communication authority under adversarial conditions** — specifically in the moments that determine whether an enterprise seller is perceived as a peer by executive buyers or as a vendor presenting a slide deck.

---

## Scoring Context Requirement

The PCI score is only valid when the call context is known. The same behaviors score differently depending on:

- **Call type** — deliberate silence is correct on an SC-led call; it's a gap on an AD-led executive review
- **Role on the call** — an AD is not scored on discovery quality if they weren't the discovery lead
- **Prior history** — a finding about a "missed opportunity" may be invalid if that opportunity was addressed in a prior call

Every audit begins with a completed intake form. Without it, the PCI score reflects pattern-matching against a generic rubric — which is not the same as an accurate read of what a seller was actually trying to accomplish.
